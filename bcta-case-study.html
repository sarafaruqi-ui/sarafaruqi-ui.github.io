Case Study · UNDP Business Call to Action

Rebuilding a global brand's front door

Leading the design, development, and rebrand of the Business Call to Action website — turning a dated, static site into a searchable, story-led platform for a network of 280+ companies working across 83 countries.

Role
Communication Specialist, UNDP
Timeline
2020 – 2021
Scope
Web · Brand · Social
Partners
Website developer and graphic designer

The starting point

Before the relaunch, BCtA's website was serving a growing global network, but it looked and behaved like a 2015-era microsite. A live embedded Twitter feed anchored the homepage, the visual identity leaned on stock imagery, and the "Meet Our Members" section was a five-logo carousel — a strange bottleneck for a network of hundreds of companies.

The BCtA homepage, archived March 2019 — hero banner, member/country/reach counters, and a Twitter feed lower on the page.

Before / after

The relaunch kept the mission-first framing BCtA was known for, but rebuilt everything underneath it: a co-branded identity with UNDP, a lighter and more editorial visual language, and a structure built to scale as the network grew.

"Meet Our Members" went from a five-logo carousel to a filterable finder covering 280 companies. It was a genuine improvement in findability — even if the paginated list that resulted still had room to feel more modern.

New, not just redesigned

Some pieces weren't a facelift — they didn't exist before. An interactive global network map replaced a static graphic, and editorial storytelling replaced the live Twitter embed as the homepage's news anchor.

The new interactive network map — click any country to see the members operating there.
Image-led editorial cards replaced the old text-list-plus-live-Twitter-feed homepage layout.

Making "About" actually explain something

The old About page tried to do everything in one scroll: mission, history, donor list, team, and a full explainer on inclusive business, all as unbroken paragraphs under a row of tiny circular icons that acted as an in-page jump menu. A visitor had to read several paragraphs before finding out what BCtA actually did.

The goal wasn't to shorten the About page — it was to make every section answer one question, so a visitor never had to read a paragraph to find out what they'd just landed on.

What changed, and why it mattered

  • About page

    Broke one long, multi-topic scroll into distinct, single-purpose sections — mission, inclusive business, team, partners — each scannable in seconds instead of requiring a full read-through.

  • Navigation

    Cut from six top-level items to four, matching how members actually moved through the site rather than how the org chart was structured. Toolkits and Impact — tools mainly used by logged-in members — were consolidated behind a single sign-on under Member Login, clearing them off the public nav entirely.

  • Visual identity

    Retired stock imagery for real member and field photography, and introduced a co-branded UNDP lockup — done in partnership with a branding agency, alongside a new brand book and tone-of-voice guide.

  • Member directory

    Replaced the five-logo carousel with a filterable member finder — searchable by SDG, sector, region, and status across 280+ companies. A real gain in findability, though the resulting paginated list was a known area we'd have liked to modernize further.

  • Impact reporting

    Moved from three flat counters to a full breakdown by Sustainable Development Goal, giving funders and partners a credible, specific story.

  • Content migration

    Migrated years of news, member profiles, and resources from the legacy CMS without losing SEO equity or breaking inbound links.

  • Social & brand system

    Extended the new identity across BCtA's social channels with a matching template system, so the website and social presence read as one brand for the first time.

A new site alone doesn't grow a membership network — but a site that's easy to navigate, easy to be found on, and easy to trust removes friction for every company deciding whether to join.

Extending the system

The new design system was built to be reused, not just admired. When BCtA launched the Inclusive Innovation Journey program, I designed its page using the templates from the relaunch — proof the new system could support new initiatives without starting from scratch each time.
